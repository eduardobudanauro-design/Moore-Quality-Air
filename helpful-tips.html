Helpful Tips for Homeowners

Get the Most From Your Home's Comfort Systems

Simple, New Mexico–specific tips to keep your heating, cooling and plumbing running well — and lower your bills along the way. When you'd rather leave it to a pro, we're a call away.

Change Your Air Filters Regularly

This is the single easiest thing you can do for your system. A dirty filter makes your furnace and AC work harder, raises your energy bill and shortens equipment life. Replace standard filters every 30 to 90 days — more often if you have pets or during dusty, windy stretches. In the Albuquerque area, spring winds can clog a filter fast, so check monthly.

Schedule Maintenance Twice a Year

Have your cooling system checked in the spring and your heating system in the fall, before you need them most. A yearly tune-up catches small problems early, keeps your equipment efficient and helps avoid breakdowns on the hottest or coldest day of the year. We recommend having all HVAC equipment inspected once a year.

Swamp Cooler Seasonal Start-Up & Shut-Down

If you run an evaporative (swamp) cooler, a little seasonal care goes a long way:

  • Spring start-up: reconnect the water line, replace the pads, clean the reservoir, and check the pump and belt before the first hot day.
  • Fall shut-down: drain the unit, disconnect and drain the water line, and cover the cooler to prevent cold air and water damage over winter.

Not sure where to start? We service and repair swamp coolers across Albuquerque, Rio Rancho, Corrales and Placitas.

Thinking About Refrigerated Air?

Many New Mexico homeowners are switching from swamp coolers to refrigerated air for more consistent comfort and lower humidity. The best time to plan a conversion is in the cooler months, before the summer rush. We do more conversions in Albuquerque than almost anyone — learn how it works.

Protect Your Pipes Before a Freeze

New Mexico nights can drop below freezing fast. Before a cold snap:

  • Disconnect garden hoses and cover outdoor hose bibs with an insulated cover.
  • Let a faucet drip on the coldest nights to keep water moving.
  • Know where your main water shut-off is — so you can act fast if a pipe bursts.

Care for Your Water Heater

Sediment build-up makes a water heater work harder and wear out sooner. Flushing the tank once a year helps it run efficiently and last longer. If you have a tankless unit, a periodic descaling keeps it delivering hot water on demand. When yours is past its prime, we install and service both tank and tankless models.

Use a Smart Thermostat

A programmable or app-based thermostat is one of the easiest ways to cut energy use without sacrificing comfort. Set it back while you're away or asleep, and control it from your phone. We can install and set one up for you.

When to Call a Pro

Filter changes and seasonal prep are great DIY tasks. But call a licensed technician for anything involving refrigerant, gas lines, electrical connections or water leaks inside walls. If you smell gas, leave the home and call your gas company first, then us.
